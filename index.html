The 2025 Women's Kabaddi World Cup (officially the 2025 World Kabaddi Cup – Women) was the second edition of the Women's Kabaddi World Cup, an international kabaddi tournament contested by women's national teams. Organised by the International Kabaddi Federation (IKF) and hosted by the Bangladesh Kabaddi Federation, the tournament took place from 17 to 24 November 2025 in Dhaka, Bangladesh. It was the first Women's Kabaddi World Cup to be held outside India.[1]

India won the tournament, defeating Chinese Taipei 35–28 in the final to claim their second consecutive title. India completed the competition unbeaten, maintaining their 100% record in Women's Kabaddi World Cup history.[2]

Background

The inaugural Women's Kabaddi World Cup was held in 2012 in Patna, Bihar, India, where India defeated Iran in the final. No further editions were organised until 2025, largely due to scheduling conflicts and the focus on Asian Games and Pro Kabaddi League commitments. In 2024, the International Kabaddi Federation announced that Bangladesh would host the 2025 edition, marking the first time a Women's World Cup was staged outside India.

Participating teams

Eleven teams from four continents participated, the highest number ever for a Women's Kabaddi World Cup.[3]

Group stage highlights

The group stage saw India dominate Group A with a perfect record, while Chinese Taipei surprised many by topping Group B ahead of the host nation, Bangladesh. The debutant teams, including Germany and Poland, displayed impressive technical growth despite lacking international experience.

Knockout stage

Semi-finals (23 November 2025)

  • India 33–21 Iran
  • Chinese Taipei 36–32 Bangladesh

Final (24 November 2025)

India 35–28 Chinese Taipei

India successfully defended their title, remaining unbeaten throughout the tournament. Sanju Devi of India was the top raider of the tournament, while the Indian defence, led by players such as Nidhi Sharma and Manisha, was instrumental in the victory. The final match was a closely contested affair until the 35th minute when India inflicted an "All-Out" to secure a decisive lead.[4]

Significance

The 2025 edition marked several milestones:

  • First Women's Kabaddi World Cup hosted outside India.
  • Highest number of participating nations (11).
  • Debut appearances by Germany, Poland, Uganda and Zanzibar at the senior women's World Cup level.
  • Chinese Taipei reached their first-ever final in any Kabaddi World Cup (men or women).

The successful hosting by Bangladesh boosted the sport's profile in South Asia and encouraged further international expansion of women's kabaddi.[6]
